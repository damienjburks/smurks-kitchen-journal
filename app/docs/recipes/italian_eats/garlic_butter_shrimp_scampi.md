## Overview

✨ A trattoria classic made easy at home!

Impress your dinner companions with this quick, elegant shrimp scampi. Juicy sautéed shrimp meet perfectly al dente linguine and crispy roasted broccoli — all tossed together in a rich garlic-lemon-Parmesan butter sauce. A hint of chili adds gentle warmth, while the bright lemon zest balances every buttery bite. Pour yourself a glass of wine, and you’ve got an effortlessly fancy dinner ready in 30 minutes.

Inspired by [Hello Fresh], this scampi proves that comfort food and sophistication can absolutely share the same bowl.

> - **Serves:** 6
> - **Prep Time:** ~10 minutes
> - **Cook Time:** ~30 minutes

## Ingredients

### Produce

- 3 lemons
- 3 heads of broccoli (about 24 oz)
- 6 cloves garlic

### Pantry

- 18 oz linguine pasta
- 2 tsp chili flakes (to taste)
- 16.5 oz seafood stock concentrate
- Olive oil
- Salt & pepper

### Dairy

- 9 tbsp butter
- 9 tbsp Parmesan cheese

### Protein

- 30 oz shrimp (peeled and deveined)

## Instructions

### Step 1: Prep & Roast the Broccoli

1. Adjust rack to top position and preheat oven to **450°F (232°C)**.
2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
3. Wash and dry produce.
4. Cut broccoli into 1-inch florets.
5. Toss on a baking sheet with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
6. Roast until browned and crispy, 12–15 minutes.

### Step 2: Cook the Pasta

1. Once water is boiling, add linguine.
2. Cook until al dente, stirring occasionally (9–11 minutes).
3. Reserve ½ cup pasta cooking water, then drain.

### Step 3: Make the Garlic Butter

1. Soften 3 tbsp butter (6 tbsp for larger batch) in a microwave-safe bowl for about 10 seconds.
2. Add lemon zest, half the Parmesan, a pinch of garlic, and a pinch of chili flakes.
3. Mash together with a fork; season with salt and pepper.

### Step 4: Cook the Shrimp

1. Rinse shrimp under cold water, then pat dry with paper towels.
2. Toss in a bowl with olive oil, remaining minced garlic, salt, and pepper.
3. Heat a large pan over medium-high heat.
4. Add shrimp and cook, stirring occasionally, until opaque and cooked through (2–4 minutes).
5. Remove from pan and set aside.

### Step 5: Build the Sauce

1. Return shrimp to the pan.
2. Add drained linguine, roasted broccoli, seafood stock concentrate, garlic butter, and ¼ cup reserved pasta water.
3. Toss until everything is coated in sauce.
4. Add lemon juice to taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.

### Step 6: Finish & Serve

1. Divide pasta between bowls.
2. Sprinkle with remaining Parmesan and chili flakes if desired.
3. Serve with lemon wedges on the side.

## Notes

- Shrimp is fully cooked when it reaches an internal temperature of **145°F (63°C)**.
- Add a splash of white wine when sautéing shrimp for extra flavor.
- Substitute zucchini or asparagus for the broccoli for a spring twist.
