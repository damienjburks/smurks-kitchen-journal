## Overview

Quick, flavorful, and weeknight-friendly! Tender pork and crisp broccoli are stir-fried in a glossy sauce of hoisin, soy, ponzu, garlic, and a pinch of sugar, then spooned over fluffy white rice. This speedy dish delivers bold, savory-sweet flavor in every bite—and it's quicker than takeout!

This recipe is inspired by [Hello Fresh], which we originally used to make this at home.

> - **Serves:** 6  
> - **Prep Time:** ~10 minutes  
> - **Cook Time:** ~30 minutes

## Ingredients

### Produce

- 3 stalks broccoli

### Pantry

- 6 tbsp soy sauce  
- 6 tbsp hoisin sauce  
- 18 ml ponzu sauce  
- 3 tsp garlic powder  
- 2 tsp sugar  
- 2.25 cups white rice  
- Olive oil  
- Salt & pepper  

### Protein

- 30 oz ground pork

## Instructions

### Step 1: Prep Ingredients

- Wash and dry produce.  
- Cut broccoli into bite-size pieces if necessary.  
- In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, hoisin sauce, ponzu sauce, half the garlic powder, and sugar.  
- Set sauce aside.

### Step 2: Cook the Rice

- In a small pot, combine white rice, water, and a pinch of salt.  
- Bring to a boil, then cover and reduce heat to low.  
- Cook until tender, 15–20 minutes.  
- Keep covered off heat until ready to serve.

### Step 3: Stir-Fry Pork & Broccoli

- Pat pork dry with paper towels.  
- Heat a drizzle of olive oil in a large pan over medium-high heat.  
- Add broccoli and cook, stirring often, until browned and tender, 4–7 minutes.  
  **TIP:** Lower heat if broccoli browns too quickly.  
- Add pork and season with salt and pepper.  
- Cook, stirring often, until pork is cooked through, 3–5 minutes.

### Step 4: Add Sauce

- Pour in the prepared sauce.  
- Cook, stirring occasionally, until slightly thickened, 1–2 minutes.  
- Remove from heat.

### Step 5: Serve

- Fluff rice with a fork.  
- Divide rice between bowls and top with pork and broccoli stir-fry.  
- Serve immediately.
